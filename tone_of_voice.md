# Tone Of Voice: Wunderwiki

## Voice

Wunderwiki sounds curious, precise, archival, and quietly delighted. It trusts the reader. It notices strangeness without shouting about it.

The site can feel faintly uncanny, but never alarmist. It should suggest a careful researcher with a soft notebook, not a conspiracy board.

## Writing Rules

- Prefer specific nouns over hype: "source page," "catalog terms," "nearby records," "field route."
- Keep CTAs exploratory and calm: "Open record," "Trace another link," "Browse the shelf."
- Treat Wikipedia as the anchor of evidence. The site curates; it does not pretend to replace the source.
- Let uncertainty stay clean: "reported," "legend," "proposed," "disputed," and "documented" are useful words.
- Keep labels short enough to scan.

## UI Copy Replacements

- "Take me somewhere stranger" -> "Trace another link"
- "Give me a rabbit hole" -> "Open a curious record"
- "Article card" -> "Encyclopedia record"
- "Back shelf nugget" -> "Marginal file"
- "Back shelf editorial" -> "Marginal shelf"
- "Open detail page" -> "Open record"
- "Featured pages" -> "Selected records"
- "Related pages" -> "See also"
- "Semantic graph" -> "Relationship map"

## Sample Tone

- "A selected index of Wikipedia pages with unusual documentary gravity."
- "Filed here because the official record is already strange enough."
- "A quiet route through neighboring pages, sorted by shared themes and recurring motifs."
- "Open the original Wikipedia source for the full public record."
