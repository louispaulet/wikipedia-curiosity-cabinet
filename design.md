# Design Manifesto: Wunderwiki

## Core Idea

Wunderwiki is a public encyclopedia page crossed with private field notes. It should feel recognizable as a Wikipedia-adjacent reading surface, then slowly reveal the cabinet: labels, marginalia, catalog marks, quiet specimen notes, and a soft sense that every page belongs to a larger, stranger archive.

## Principles

- Readability first. Article pages must be calm, scannable, and text-led, with an encyclopedia rhythm before any atmosphere appears.
- Curiosity second. Discovery should feel like opening drawers in a well-kept cabinet, not like entering a haunted attraction.
- Paranormal without panic. Use uncertainty, archival language, and gentle annotations instead of horror tropes.
- Evidence over spectacle. The Wikipedia source link, related pages, scores, and collections should look like reference apparatus.
- Quiet depth. Texture, borders, and labels should reward attention without making the interface busy.

## Visual Language

- Surfaces: warm paper, ivory panels, light parchment, and faint ruled-note backgrounds.
- Ink: graphite and deep blue-gray for primary text; classic link blue for Wikipedia echoes.
- Accents: muted sepia, olive, brass, and faded blue, used sparingly.
- Borders: thin rules, double lines, table borders, and notebook dividers rather than glowing frames.
- Typography: serif headings with system sans UI, inspired by encyclopedia pages and catalog records.
- Shape: mostly square or lightly rounded corners. Avoid oversized pill/card softness except for compact controls.
- Motion: subtle hover lifts and color shifts only. No dramatic glow.

## Layout Rules

- Home should read like an index page: lead article, table-of-contents feeling, featured shelves, and catalog records.
- Article pages should be the most Wikipedia-like: title, intro, section rules, source citation, metadata sidebar, "See also" links.
- Collections should resemble category/archive pages, with list density and clear page counts.
- Graph and trail tools should feel like field-research instruments: maps, pins, index cards, and route notes.
- Use marginalia for mood, never as a replacement for content.

## What To Avoid

- Horror-house visuals, jump-scare language, blood reds, heavy black backgrounds, and occult overload.
- Marketing hero tropes that make the site feel like a product landing page.
- Overly decorative cards nested inside cards.
- Mystery copy that hides what a control actually does.
